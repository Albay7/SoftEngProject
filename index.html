Hardware Design Engineer

Job Description

Hardware Design Engineers play an increasingly important role in modern business. They create and design an extensive range of computer hardware components that may be used throughout the business for both internal and external interactions. The majority of their work is completed in the research laboratory. Hardware Designers are in a technically demanding role that requires a scientific and mathematical brain to accompany creative flair and a strong understanding of the tech challenges facing modern businesses. It additionally requires meticulous attention to detail to all elements of design, deployment, and subsequent troubleshooting and maintenance. The job role has evolved in more recent times, transcending the concept of working exclusively on computers to cover the realms of IoT technologies, cell phone devices, tablets, and even vehicles. The processes of treating both analog and digital circuitry can embody every stage from design through to analyzing data.

Educational Requirements

To become a Hardware Design Engineer, the typical educational path begins with a Bachelor’s degree in a related field, such as Electrical Engineering (EE), Computer Engineering, or Electronic Engineering. This foundational degree provides essential knowledge in digital and analog circuit design, embedded systems, microcontroller programming, PCB design, and other core topics like signal processing and power electronics. A Bachelor's degree equips students with the theoretical and practical skills required for designing and developing hardware systems, making it the minimum educational requirement for entering the profession.

While a Master’s degree is not mandatory, it can significantly enhance career prospects and expertise, especially for engineers aiming to specialize in areas such as ASIC design, FPGA programming, or high-speed digital design. A Master's program delves deeper into advanced topics like System-on-Chip (SoC) design, signal integrity, and power management, and provides opportunities for research or involvement in cutting-edge projects.

Duties and Responsibilities

Hardware design engineers play a crucial role in developing and improving electronic systems. They are responsible for designing and testing circuit boards, ensuring signal integrity, and integrating various components into a cohesive system.

  1. Perform back-end flow from RTL code synthesis, timing driven physical design to achieve a timing closure type-out database.
  2. Lead design team through preliminary and detail design, including trade studies, requirement verification, system prototyping and testing.
  3. Develop hardware and user-friendly GUI driven software to automate the manufacturing test and debug of microprocessor/ DSP control VXI bus modules.
  4. Generate synthesis reports and automate simulation using Perl scripts.
  5. Implement SPI port, USB port and Ethernet port.

Salary

The salary of a Hardware Design Engineer in the Philippines varies based on experience, location, and industry. Entry-level engineers earn between PHP 20,000 to PHP 40,000 per month, while those with 3 to 5 years of experience can expect salaries between PHP 40,000 to PHP 70,000. Senior engineers with over five years of experience typically earn between PHP 70,000 to PHP 120,000, and highly experienced engineers in specialized or managerial roles can make upwards of PHP 120,000 to PHP 180,000 or more. Salaries tend to be higher in larger cities like Metro Manila and Cebu, and in industries such as semiconductors and telecommunications.
