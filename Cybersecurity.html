CYBERSECURITY

Course Description

The Bachelor of Cybersecurity is a program that focuses on protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. This course helps the learner to have a deeper understanding of modern information and system security protection technologies and methods. These include an in-depth understanding of the different types of cyberattacks, threats and different technology to use to do the attack and how to mitigate those attacks focusing on the systems are most at risk, and importance of an organization-wide approach to cybersecurity.

The Cybersecurity course focuses on protecting computer systems, networks, and data from cyber threats, attacks, and unauthorized access. It prepares students to identify vulnerabilities, prevent breaches, and respond to incidents effectively. It is ideal for those interested in technology and problem-solving with a focus on security.

What to learn

Students in a Cybersecurity course gain expertise in areas such as:

  • Network Security: Understanding firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and virtual private networks (VPNs).
  • Ethical Hacking: Learning how to think like hackers to test and strengthen security systems.
  • Cybersecurity Tools: Mastering tools like Wireshark, Metasploit, and Kali Linux for security analysis.
  • Threat Analysis: Identifying potential risks, malware, phishing, and other cyber threats.
  • Compliance and Legal Issues: Understanding privacy laws and regulations like GDPR and HIPAA.

The course often includes hands-on labs, simulations, and real-world case studies to ensure students can apply their knowledge effectively in professional settings.
